# his500_settlement-of-the-upper-canada-frontier
Two geopackage GIS files. One of European homesteader settlement of Upper Canada (present-day Ontario) from 1790 to 1851. Another of First Nations territories in the year 1837.

Note on territorial map: The shapefiles had a lot of cuts that represented roads and railways. I found no evidence from the treaties that these originally existed. Rather, they appear to be formed sometime after 1837, so they have been filled in. The changed borders are with the Stony Reserve, the Bay of Quinete Reserve and the Six Nations of the Grand RIver. Note also the Oneida Nation of the Thames did not exist until 1840. Other land claims besides the Six Nations, Quinete Bay and Stony Creek were done using this: https://geo.sac-isc.gc.ca/cirrp-scsim/index-eng.html?wbdisable=true Also note that the St. Clair and Stony Creek settlements were one reserve in 1919, but may split this if the troop data requires this. Same for the Delaware and Thames reserves, which split at an unspecified date. Caldwell First Nation did not have land until 2020: https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/windsor/caldwell-first-nation-approves-land-claim-offer-1.975428 (for fact they did not have land) https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/windsor/caldwell-first-nation-reserve-status-1.5955097 (for where they were given land).
